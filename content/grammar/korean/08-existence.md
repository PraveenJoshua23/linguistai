---
id: existence
title: Existence 있다 / 없다
level: Beginner
summary: Use 있다 and 없어요 to say something exists or does not exist.
examples:
  - text: 책상 위에 책이 있어요.
    romanization: chaeksang wi-e chaegi isseoyo.
    translation: There is a book on the desk.
  - text: 냉장고 안에 우유가 없어요.
    romanization: naengjanggo an-e uyuga eopseoyo.
    translation: There is no milk in the refrigerator.
  - text: 우리 집에 고양이가 있어요.
    romanization: uri jib-e goyang-i-ga isseoyo.
    translation: There is a cat in our house.
exercises:

    options:

      - 없다
    answerIndex: 0
    explanation: Use 있어요 to state that the pen exists.
  - prompt: 냉장고에 우유 ___ .
    promptTranslation: There is milk in the refrigerator.
    options:
      - 없어요
      - 있어요
      - 없다
      - 있다
    answerIndex: 0
    explanation: >-

  - prompt: 우리 집에 고양이 ___ .
    promptTranslation: There is a cat in our house.
    options:
      - 없어요
      - 있어요
      - 없다
      - 있다
    answerIndex: 0
    explanation: 없어요 is used to say the cat does not exist in the house.
  - prompt: 공원에 나무 ___ .
    promptTranslation: There are trees in the park.

      - 있다

    answerIndex: 0

---
있다 means "to exist" or "to have" and is used with the polite ending 있어요. 없어요 is the negative form meaning "does not exist" or "does not have." Place the verb after the location or object you are talking about. The sentence ends with the verb, not a particle.
