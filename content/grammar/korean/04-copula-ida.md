title: The Copula 이다 (to be)
level: Beginner
summary: Use 이에요/예요 to say "A is B" in polite Korean.
examples:
  - text: 저는 학생이에요.
    romanization: jeoneun haksaeng-ieyo.
    translation: I am a student.
  - text: 이 책은 재미있어요.
    romanization: i chaegeun jaemiisseoyo.
    translation: This book is interesting.
  - text: 서울은 한국의 수도예요.
    romanization: Seoureun hangukui sudo-yeyo.
    translation: Seoul is the capital of Korea.
exercises:

---
Place the copula after the noun that describes something. If the noun ends with a consonant, use 이에요; if it ends with a vowel, use 예요. This form is polite and works in everyday sentences. The ending stays attached directly to the noun.
