id: past-tense
title: Past Tense -았/었어요
level: Beginner
summary: 'Polite past tense in Korean is formed with -았어요, -었어요, or -했어요.'
examples:
  - text: 나는 어제 책을 읽었어요.
    romanization: Naneun eoje chaekeul ilgeosseoyo.
    translation: I read a book yesterday.
  - text: 그녀는 밥을 먹었어요.
    romanization: Geunyeoneun babeul meogeosseoyo.
    translation: She ate rice.
  - text: 우리는 영화를 봤어요.
    romanization: Urineun yeonghwareul bwasseoyo.
    translation: We watched a movie.
exercises:
  - prompt: 어제 친구와 같이 카페에 ___ .
    promptTranslation: I ___ at a cafe with a friend yesterday.
    options:
      - 갔어요
      - 갔었어요
      - 가셨어요
      - 가요
    answerIndex: 0
    explanation: Use -았어요 after the vowel ㄱ to form the past polite.
  - prompt: 그는 매일 운동을 ___ .
    promptTranslation: He ___ exercise every day.
    options:
      - 했어요
      - 하셨어요
      - 했었어요
      - 해요
    answerIndex: 0
    explanation: 'For 하다, the past polite is -했어요.'
  - prompt: 나는 아침에 일찍 ___ .
    promptTranslation: I ___ early in the morning.
    options:
      - 일어났어요
      - 일어났어요?
      - 일어났어요!
      - 일어났어요.
    answerIndex: 0
    explanation: The verb 일어나다 takes -았어요 because the stem ends with ㄴ (a consonant).
  - prompt: 그녀는 시험을 ___ .
    promptTranslation: She ___ the exam.
    options:
      - 봤어요
      - 보셨어요
      - 보았어요
      - 보았어요?
    answerIndex: 0
    explanation: 'After the vowel ㅂ, use -았어요 for the past polite.'
---
To make a verb polite past, add -았어요 after a vowel, -었어요 after a consonant, and -했어요 after 하다. The choice depends on the verb stem's final sound. This form is used in everyday conversation. Remember the vowel harmony: 아 afterㅏ orㅗ, 어 after other vowels.
