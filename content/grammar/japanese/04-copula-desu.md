---
id: copula-desu
title: The Copula です
level: Beginner
summary: 'です is the polite copula meaning “to be,” and its negative form is じゃありません.'
examples:
  - text: これは本です。
    romanization: Kore wa hon desu.
    translation: This is a book.
  - text: 彼は学生です。
    romanization: Kare wa gakusei desu.
    translation: He is a student.
  - text: それは高くありません。
    romanization: Sore wa takaku arimasen.
    translation: That is not expensive.

---
In polite Japanese, you attach です after a noun or adjective to say something is that thing. To make it negative, replace です with じゃありません. The sentence ends with the copula, so no extra verb is needed.
