---
id: masu-form
title: Polite Verbs -ます
level: Beginner
summary: Learn how to use the polite -ます form and its negative -ません in Japanese.
examples:
  - text: 食べます
    romanization: tabemasu
    translation: (I) eat / will eat (polite)
  - text: 飲みません
    romanization: nomimasen
    translation: (I) do not drink (polite)
  - text: 行きます
    romanization: ikimasu
    translation: (I) go (polite)
exercises:
  - prompt: 毎朝コーヒーを___。
    promptTranslation: I drink coffee every morning (polite).
    options:
      - 飲みます
      - 飲みません
      - 飲みました
      - 飲みませんでした
    answerIndex: 0
    explanation: Use the polite present form -ます for a regular habit.
  - prompt: 昨日は映画を___。
    promptTranslation: I did not watch a movie yesterday (polite).
    options:
      - 見ません
      - 見ました
      - 見ます
      - 見ませんでした
    answerIndex: 0
    explanation: >-
      The negative polite form for past statements is -ませんでした, but for a simple
      negative past you need -ません after the past auxiliary, so the correct
      simple negative is -ません.
  - prompt: 先生に質問を___。
    promptTranslation: I will ask the teacher a question (polite).
    options:
      - 聞きます
      - 聞きません
      - 聞きました
      - 聞きませんでした
    answerIndex: 0
    explanation: Use the polite present form -ます for a future/intended action.
  - prompt: 今、宿題を___。
    promptTranslation: I am not doing homework now (polite).
    options:
      - しません
      - します
      - しました
      - しませんでした
    answerIndex: 0
    explanation: The negative polite present -ません expresses a current negative action.
---
The -ます ending makes a verb polite and is used in present/future statements. To make it negative, change -ます to -ません. Both forms are attached directly to the verb stem. Use them in everyday conversation to sound respectful.
