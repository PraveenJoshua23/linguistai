---
id: adjectives
title: い and な Adjectives
level: Intermediate
summary: >-
  Japanese adjectives are divided into い‑adjectives and な‑adjectives, each with
  its own attachment and conjugation rules.
examples:
  - text: この山は高いです。
    romanization: Kono yama wa takai desu.
    translation: This mountain is tall.
  - text: 彼は元気な学生です。
    romanization: Kare wa genki na gakusei desu.
    translation: He is a lively student.
  - text: この部屋は静かです。
    romanization: Kono heya wa shizuka desu.
    translation: This room is quiet.
exercises:
  - prompt: この山は高___ないです。
    promptTranslation: This mountain is not tall.
    options:
      - く
      - さ
      - な
      - い
    answerIndex: 0
    explanation: The negative of an い‑adjective is formed by replacing い with くない.
  - prompt: 彼は ___ 学生です。
    promptTranslation: He is a ___ student.
    options:
      - 元気な
      - 元気の
      - 元気で
      - 元気
    answerIndex: 0
    explanation: な‑adjectives attach to nouns with な.
  - prompt: 今日は ___ 天気です。
    promptTranslation: The weather today is ___ .
    options:
      - いい
      - よい
      - よく
      - いいく
    answerIndex: 0
    explanation: い‑adjectives directly modify nouns without additional particles.
  - prompt: この部屋は静か___ないです。
    promptTranslation: This room is not quiet.
    options:
      - で
      - な
      - が
      - は
    answerIndex: 0
    explanation: The negative of a な‑adjective uses ではない.
---
い‑adjectives end with い and can directly modify nouns (e.g., 高い山). To make them negative, replace い with くない. な‑adjectives need the particle な before a noun (e.g., 静かな部屋) and use ではない for the negative form. Both types change similarly for past and polite forms.
